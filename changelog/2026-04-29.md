## Trends & Observations

1. **Feed-forward 3DGS density control**: SparseSplat (CVPR 2026) introduces adaptive Gaussian density — a clear signal that future feed-forward methods will move beyond uniform Gaussian maps
2. **Self-evolving reconstruction**: Free Geometry shows LoRA-based self-refinement can improve any feed-forward model at test time, blurring the line between feed-forward and per-scene optimization
3. **Training-free simplification gains traction**: NanoGS proves KNN merge without retraining is viable, complementing the compression trend (OT-UVGS, Gaussians on a Diet)
4. **Cross-domain GS expansion**: GS-DOT adapts GS to medical imaging (photon diffusion), expanding GS beyond visual rendering
5. **Egocentric gap identified**: First benchmark reveals dynamic 3DGS degrades on egocentric video — opens new research direction
